ABOUT SEKONDI TAKORADI TECHNICAL INSTITUTE

THE HISTORICAL JOURNEY

Established on September 22nd, 1998, under the visionary leadership of His Excellency John Evans Fiifi Atta Mills, the Sekondi-Takoradi Technical Institute (STTI), formerly known as Takoradi Vocational Training Institute (TVTI), has stood as a bastion of technical education in the Western Region of Ghana. It was the first Institution in the Western Region to be established under the then National Vocational Training Institute (NVTI). Conceived through a collaborative effort between the government of Ghana and the Department for International Development (DFID) project, STTI was envisioned to provide the best employable Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) skills to the youth of the region. This led to the formation of TVTI in the Western Region of Ghana. In 2021, a significant milestone was achieved as Takoradi Vocational Training Institute (TVTI) underwent a transformation, emerging as the Sekondi-Takoradi Technical Institute. This transition, mandated by the government's Pre-Tertiary Education Act, 2020 (Act 1049), marked a new chapter in the institute's journey, aligning it with the evolving educational landscape and establishing it under the Ghana Technical and Vocational Education and Training Service (Ghana TVET Service).

PROGRAMS OFFERED BY THE SCHOOL

The initial programmes during the institute’s establishment in 1998 were Auto Mechanic, Welding and Fabrication, Autobody, Mechanical Engineering (Machining) and General Electrical. Over the years Auto mechanic was split into Motor Vehicle Electricals & Electronic and Motor Vehicle Mechanics. Information Communication Technology (ICT) programme was also added in the year 2012. Currently at STTI, a diverse array of programs awaits aspiring students, encompassing fields such as Motor Vehicle Technology (both Electrical and Mechanical aspect of vehicles), Electrical/Electronics Engineering Technology, Mechanical Engineering Technology, Welding and Fabrication Technology, Information Communication Technology, Refrigeration & Air Condition Technology and Plumbing & Gas Fitting Technology. However, the institute curriculum is meticulously designed to meet industry demands and equip graduates with the requisite skills for both employment and entrepreneurship. Notably, the institute is seriously looking for partners for collaboration to start an Oil & Gas Technology Training section, to cater for the specialized needs of the burgeoning energy sector within the Western Region.


SHORT COURSES OFFERED BY THE SCHOOL

In addition to its comprehensive programs, STTI offers short courses and apprenticeship opportunities tailored to the needs of various stakeholders. From informal short courses for job seekers to specialized training sessions for corporate institutions, the institute remains committed to fostering skill development and capacity building across diverse sectors. Over the years, STTI has collaborated with national and international organizations, including JICA, KOICA, GIZ among others to offer various training programmes especially to females. These collaborations underscore its commitment to innovation and global best practices in technical education. The accolades and achievements of STTI are a testament to its unwavering dedication to excellence and inclusivity. From training initiatives under the Youth Inclusive Entrepreneurial Development initiative for Employment (YIEDIE) program to partnerships with esteemed organizations such as the European Union (ILO project) and World University Service of Canada (INVEST IN HER project), the institute continues to make significant contributions to youth empowerment and economic development.

Understanding the School Logo

The various symbols in our school logo are uniquely explained below:

The Engineering Wheel signifies that STTI is fundamentally an engineering training school. We transform unskilled individuals into skilled engineers. The human figure holding tools represents the journey from novice to expert, as students evolve into master craftspeople skilled in their chosen fields. Many graduates have established their own businesses or secured roles in industries such as mining, oil, ports, and the Ghana Armed Forces. Others have pursued further education, advancing to the tertiary level.

The Book with a Light signifies that our training institute is a Centre for acquiring knowledge. Aside from acquiring skills, STTI equips people with knowledge in areas such as Mathematics, Science, ICT, Entrepreneurship, Social Studies, and English Language, preparing our students for the workforce and ensuring successful living as well as academic progression into higher education.

The Blue Sea with an Oil Drop indicates the location of the Institute (Takoradi, the Oil City) around the seashore and in an oil-rich region of Ghana. This location affords the institute special privileges, including immediate employment after school, partnerships with various industries, and the benefit of enjoying city life.

Colors in the Logo:

  • Blue represents TVET, producing "blue-collar" jobs.
  • Gold signifies the wealth produced by STTI or TVET.

STTI was established in 1998. The logo conveys the message: “Nsano Ndwuma Hen Mpuntu” (Skills acquisition brings about economic benefits). This means that the skilled individuals produced by STTI or TVET in the oil city of Takoradi will propel development and wealth for themselves, their communities, Ghana, and the world at large.

OBJECTIVE OF GHANA TVET SERVICE

The object of service is to manage, oversee and implement approved national policies and programmes relating to pre-tertiary technical and vocational education and training.With the establishment of the Ghana TVET Service under the Pre-Tertiary Education Act, 2020 (Act 1049), all TVET providing institutions under nine (9) ministries (GES - TVED, DICCES, NVTI, OIC, Social Welfare Training Centre, Youth Leadership Training Institutes, GRATIS, Technology, Government Technical Training Centre, Community Development Vocational Institute) have been realigned to the Ghana TVET Service under Ministry of Education.
